{% trans 'How will my parcel be delivered?' %}

Shipping Carrier: Different shipping carriers (e.g., yalidine express, kazitour, DHL, USPS) have their own delivery methods and networks. The carrier used for your parcel will affect the delivery process. Service Level: The service level you selected when sending the parcel can determine how it will be delivered. For example, express shipping services often provide faster delivery than standard services. Destination: The destination of your parcel (e.g., local, domestic, international) can impact the delivery method and transit times. Tracking: If your parcel has a tracking number, you can often monitor its progress and delivery status through the carrier's tracking system. Delivery Address: The delivery address you provided plays a crucial role in how your parcel will be delivered. Ensure it is accurate and complete. Special Instructions: Some parcels may have special delivery instructions, such as requiring a signature upon delivery or specific handling requirements. To find out more about the delivery of your specific parcel, you should refer to the tracking information provided by the shipping carrier or contact the sender or the carrier's customer service for assistance. They can provide you with the most up-to-date information about your parcel's delivery status and estimated delivery date.

Do I pay for delivery?

Yes, you generally need to pay for delivery or shipping when ordering a product or sending a parcel. The cost depends on factors like shipping method, parcel size, distance, and carrier. You can choose from various shipping options, each with different fees. International shipping is usually more expensive than domestic. Additional services like insurance or expedited delivery may incur extra charges. Review shipping costs during checkout, and look for promotions or discounts. Payment is typically made when placing your order or sending the parcel, and details can be found on the retailer's or carrier's website or through customer service.
